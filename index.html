The MIT   Vaccines for All   Conference

COVID-19 Digital Tools to Empower Citizens

December 11, 2020 | 9:00 AM EST - 2:00 PM EST

Register

Designing digital solutions which engage citizens in four core areas — logistics, health outcomes, user-centric impact, and communication.

Speakers

Disparities in equitable distribution, vaccine efficacy, duration of immunity, multi-dose adherence, and privacy-focused user experience are among the most critical difficulties that must be addressed. The biggest hurdles in designing digital solutions which engage citizens in this pandemic are human and societal as well as technological. Trust at all levels and among all participants is mandatory for success. Can participatory real time epidemiology and user-centric crowd-sourced solutions help? The deployment of exposure notification/health passport apps is a promising start.



The conference will bring together experts and stakeholders to address the consequences impacting disease-spread, individual behaviour, society, the economy, and data privacy. Speakers include the below and many more!

Call for Spotlight

The MIT “Vaccines for All”: COVID-19 Digital Tools to Empower Citizens conference invites abstract submissions in all areas related (but not limited to): vaccine logistics, health outcomes, challenges, and consequences. To create a constructive, innovative, and open community, all quality abstracts will be welcomed to present at the presentation session. Presenting an abstract is a great way to receive feedback for both published and unpublished work. We highly encourage abstract based on published work. Abstract presentations are an integral part of the conference and will be presented in interactive sessions. Additionally, abstracts will be distributed to all conference participants and virtually presented at 1 PM ET on December 11th.

Due to the virtual nature of the abstract presentation this year, we recommend abstracts be submitted in the slides presentation format.

Abstracts should be limited to maximum of 4 pages (single column) for technical content including figures and possible references, and with one additional optional 5th page containing only references.

Deadline for submission: December 7
Submission Instructions: http://vaccine.mit.edu/presentation-instructions
Presentation Template: Latex and Word
Presentation Notification: December 9


The MIT "Vaccines for All": COVID-19 Digital Tools to Empower Citizens conference invites essay submissions pertinent to vaccination and the COVID-19 global pandemic. We highly encourage everyone to seize this opportunity to learn, write, and discuss about COVID-19 vaccination. We recommend utilizing sources to help you in your research, but make sure to use your imagination and think outside the box! All quality essays will be presented at the essay session that will take place at 1 PM ET on December 11th.
